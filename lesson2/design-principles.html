Alignment

Big Hit (BTS The Most Beautiful Moment in Life Pt. 2)

https://ibighit.com/bts/eng/discography/detail/hwayangyeonhwa-pt2.html

The Track list on the bottom is a great example of alignment. Each of the numbers are in horizontal alignment. They then have a nice use of white space between the numbers and the track names. This and the color change show that they are not one thing, but define each other. The track names have a left alignment which create a clean apparence. This makes it easier to read than if they had say a center alignment.

Contrast

Big Hit (BTS Map of the Soul 7)

https://ibighit.com/bts/eng/discography/detail/map_of_the_soul-7.html

Here we have a wonderful example of contrast. The white text over top of the blue background aids in easy reading for the user. The different font sizes add to the contrast as well as visual hierarchy, telling the reader where to look next. You see a little of the text at the bottom that is not as bright white as the beginning text and that helps to define that it is not as important as the text before.

Whitespace

Become (Come Unto Christ)

https://become.comeuntochrist.org/

This shot of the Become website is a great example of the use of white space. Nothing feels cramped together. They gave each section room to itself. This is especially important on mobile as you have to give space for the buttons to be pressed. They also added a good amount of padding to the sign up button and between it and the sign in button. This makes for a user friendly experience.
